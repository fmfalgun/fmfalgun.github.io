Reliable Authentication of IoT Using Kerberos and Blockchain

Presents an upgraded Kerberos authentication system integrating blockchain to address centralised single-point failure and scalability limitations. Proposes decentralised authentication via symmetric-to-asymmetric cryptography migration, enabling fault-tolerant, high-security IoT data sharing. Includes formal mathematical proof of scheme correctness and comparative analysis against existing IoT authentication approaches.

Springer Feb 2026 Kerberos Blockchain IoT Auth
2025

A Comprehensive Survey on Zero Trust Architecture in IoT Networks

In-depth analysis of Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) adoption in IoT environments. Examines limitations of traditional perimeter-based models and evaluates ZT principles — continuous authentication, least privilege, micro-segmentation, and continuous monitoring — against IoT constraints including device heterogeneity, scalability, and resource limitations. Reviews enabling technologies and AI/ML-driven anomaly detection, with future directions in lightweight cryptography and autonomous policy orchestration.

IEEE Dec 2025 Zero Trust IoT Security Survey
